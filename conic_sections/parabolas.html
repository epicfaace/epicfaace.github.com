Parabolas

Definition

A parabola is a set of points which are equidistant from the directrix (line) and the focus (point).

The standard form of a parabola is:

$$\textrm{Function: (y=...) } 4p(y-k)=(x-h)^{2}$$ $$\textrm{Not a function: (x=...) } 4p(x-h)=(y-k)^{2}$$

$p$ is the focal length, or the length between the vertex and the directrix/focus.

The Lotus Rectum is a line segment which passes through the focus and has both endpoints on the curve. It is equal to $4p$.

$$\textrm{Vertex: } (h,k)$$ $$ \textrm{Directrix: } \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} &y=V(y)-p &&\textrm{if y=...}\\ &x=V(x)-p &&\textrm{if x=...} \end{array} \right.$$ $$ \textrm{Focus: } \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} &(V(x),V(y)+p) &&\textrm{if y=...}\\ &(V(x)+p,V(y)) &&\textrm{if x=...} \end{array} \right.$$ $$ \textrm{Axis of Symmetry: } \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} &x=V(x) &&\textrm{if y=...}\\ &y=V(y) &&\textrm{if x=...} \end{array} \right.$$

Graphing

This has a $\textrm{x=...}$ form:

$$(y+2)^{2}=4(x-1)$$ $$\textrm{Vertex: }(1,-2)$$ $$4p=4; p=1\textrm{ and }LR=4$$ $$\textrm{Focus: }(2,-2)$$ $$\textrm{Directrix: }x=0$$ $$\textrm{AoS: }y=-2$$
  1. Graph the vertex.
  2. Graph the Focus.
  3. Draw two points $\pm \frac{LR}{2}$ away from the focus.
  4. Connect the vertex and the two points to draw the shape!
