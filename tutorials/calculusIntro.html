Calculus

$\frac{dy}{dx}$ is the derivative of a function $y$, which is the slope!

$$\frac{dy}{dx}\rightarrow\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\rightarrow\frac{\textrm{Change in }y}{\textrm{Change in }x}\rightarrow\textrm{Slope}$$

For example, if $y=2x+3$, $\frac{dy}{dx}$ (or $\frac{d}{dx}y$) (or $y'$) is 2.

Slope is really easy with linear equations, but what about equations such as $f(x)=x^2$? This is when calculus comes into play.
